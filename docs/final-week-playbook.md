# Final-Week & Exam-Day Playbook (research brief)

Cross-checked across College Board, Kaplan, Princeton Review, PrepScholar, Test
Innovators, Piqosity/IvyStrides (adaptive mechanics), Pursu (Desmos), PrepMaven,
The Critical Reader, and test-prep community consensus. This is the source
material for the in-app "📋 Exam-Day Playbook" section.

## 1 · Structure the final week (spacing beats cramming)
- The proctored mock ~4 days out is the LAST full test; no full-length test within 48h of test day.
- Until the mock: daily 45–60 min targeted drills from the error log. Twice-missed topics = system problem → drill first.
- After the mock: 1 day deep review (repeat errors only), then short mixed drills.
- Day before: 20–30 min light review max; never a new topic this week.
- End each day with retrieval (redo missed questions cold), not rereading notes.

## 2 · Time budgets
- R&W (27q/32min ≈ 71s avg): ~85–90s reading questions (first half), ~45s grammar/transitions. Checkpoints: Q9 ≈ 21 min left, Q18 ≈ 10 min left.
- Math (22q/35min ≈ 95s avg): "12 and 24" rule — starting Q12, clock ≈ 24 min. Easy→hard order: bank time early.
- 30s with no path → letter-of-the-day, flag, move on. Never blank. Return within the module.

## 3 · Adaptive Module 1 → 2
- ~18–19/27 RW and ~13–16/22 Math in Module 1 routes to hard Module 2 (unofficial). Easy M2 caps ~590–650.
- Full flag-and-return sweep before M1 ends. Hard M2 feeling brutal = good news.

## 4 · Verbal procedures
- Question first, then passage. Predict before peeking (WiC: your own word; transitions: name the relationship, sort same-direction vs opposite; twin choices eliminate each other).
- Punctuation tree: both sides independent → period ≡ semicolon ≡ comma+FANBOYS (two equals offered = both wrong). Colon/dash need a complete sentence before. Interrupters take matched pairs. Comma vs nothing → usually nothing (never split subject–verb / verb–object).
- Synthesis: goal first, match the goal's exact verb, fact-check winner against notes.
- Structure/Purpose: eliminate by the choice's first verb (describe ≠ argue ≠ critique); part-vs-whole trap.
- Main idea: kill too-narrow / too-broad / opposite / extreme.
- Evidence/Quotation: underline the claim first; #1 trap is "true but irrelevant".

## 5 · Math procedures
- Desmos: both sides of any equation as y= → intersection dots = solutions; parabolas show zeros/vertex; slider k for "how many solutions"; `y1 ~ mx1 + b` regression; table backsolve.
- Backsolve from B/C; plug in easy numbers (x=2, 100 for percents).
- Re-read the final question (intermediate-value trap is always offered).
- Percents = multipliers (×(1±p/100)); successive changes multiply; reverse percent = divide.
- Circles: complete the square; center signs FLIP; arc/sector = one proportion (angle/360).
- Polynomials: (x−a) factor ⇔ zero at a; remainder ÷(x−a) = p(a); even multiplicity touches, odd crosses.
- Two-way tables: "of / among / given" fixes the denominator (that row/column, not grand total).
- Exponents: same base first; multiply→add, power→multiply, divide→subtract; fraction = root, negative = reciprocal.
- Solids: reference sheet first; ×k lengths ⇒ ×k² areas ⇒ ×k³ volumes.
- Trig: label opposite/adjacent from the QUESTION's angle; sin x = cos(90−x); 3-4-5 / 5-12-13 / 45-45-90 / 30-60-90 before Pythagoras.

## 6 · Night before & morning of
- Bluebook setup done days before; device 100% + charger; ID + admission ticket.
- Stop by ~8pm; 8h sleep two nights running; wake 2–2.5h before start; protein+carb breakfast; usual caffeine only; 10–15 min warm-up (3 easy RW + 3 easy Math).

## 7 · Sacrifice strategy
- Math: ~15–17 of 22 attempted carefully beats rushing 22. Letter-of-the-day the 5–7 hardest.
- Sunk time is never a reason to stay on a question.

## Distractor taxonomy
- R&W: too narrow / too broad / opposite / extreme / true-but-irrelevant / recycled words with twisted meaning / half-right.
- Math: intermediate step (x when asked 2x) / sign flip / wrong denominator/base / formula swap / unscaled k vs k²·k³ / inverted ratio.
